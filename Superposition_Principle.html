Superposition Principle

When two identical waves traveling in opposite directions meet, they interfere to form a standing wave.

Amplitude (\(A\)) 50

The Mathematics

Two identical waves traveling in opposite directions:

\( y_1 = A \sin(kx - \omega t) \)
\( y_2 = A \sin(kx + \omega t) \)

Using the trigonometric identity:

\( \sin(\alpha) + \sin(\beta) = 2\sin(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2})\cos(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}) \)

The total standing wave is:

\( y_{total} = 2A \sin(kx) \cos(\omega t) \)
Nodes are points that never move. They occur where \( \sin(kx) = 0 \).

\( kx = n\pi \)
Since \( k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \), then \( \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}x = n\pi \)
Position of nodes: \( x = \frac{n\lambda}{2} \)
